Social Media Code of Conduct

Effective Date: October 25, 2024

This Social Media Code of Conduct (“Code”) applies to all employees, consultants, affiliates, and representatives of Sahel Politica. Its purpose is to ensure that all interactions and communications reflect the company’s values and maintain high standards of professionalism and ethics.

  1. General Principles

    • Professionalism: Every online interaction should reflect the professionalism and integrity that Sahel Politica embodies.

    • Respect and Ethics: Communications must remain respectful, free of discriminatory, defamatory, or offensive language.

    • Transparency and Authenticity: Be clear about your role and opinions. Avoid impersonating clients or users if you’re acting on behalf of Sahel Politica.

  2. Use of Sahel Politica Official Accounts

    • Official Posts: Only authorized individuals may post content on behalf of Sahel Politica on official accounts.

    • Content Standards: Content must align with Sahel Politica’s values, accurately represent its services, and be approved by the communications team.

    • Comment Moderation: Comments on posts must be monitored to remove any inappropriate or harmful content. Constructive criticism can remain, but offensive or off-topic comments will be removed.

  3. Guidelines for Employees and Affiliates

    • Account Differentiation: Employees should clarify when speaking on their own behalf or as a Sahel Politica representative. If posting personally, clearly state that views do not reflect those of the company.

    • Confidentiality: Never share confidential or sensitive information about Sahel Politica, its clients, projects, or partners.

    • Compliance with Company Policies: Employees must follow all company privacy and data protection policies.

  4. Appropriate Content and Language

    • Respect for Diversity: Avoid any language that could be seen as discriminatory or offensive.

    • Accuracy: All shared information must be accurate and fact-based. Any erroneous or misleading information should be corrected promptly.

    • Positive and Constructive Language: Avoid making personal criticisms or negative statements, especially about competitors or other organizations.

  5. Managing Interactions with Users

    • Responsiveness: User messages and comments should be acknowledged and responded to quickly, typically within 24 working hours.

    • Respecting Diverse Opinions: Even when disagreements arise, user opinions should be respected. Respond to criticism constructively and respectfully.

    • Reporting Inappropriate Content: If a user posts inappropriate or offensive content, it will be moderated or removed following Sahel Politica’s and platform policies.

  6. Brand Protection

    • Logo and Brand Use: All usage of the Sahel Politica logo, images, or videos must be approved by management.

    • Account Security: Passwords and access information for official accounts should remain confidential and protected. Report any security issues immediately.

    • Impersonation: If an account or post appears to impersonate Sahel Politica, employees should report it to management promptly.

  7. Guidelines for Content Sharing

    • Third-Party Content: Ensure permission is obtained before sharing or reposting third-party content. Always credit the original creator.

    • No Investment Advice: Avoid any statements that could be interpreted as investment advice or financial recommendations.

    • Service Representation: Any content explaining or illustrating Sahel Politica services must be accurate and communications team-approved.

  8. Sending and Receiving Direct Messages

    • Professional Responses: Direct messages sent from Sahel Politica accounts should remain professional and respectful.

    • Confidentiality: Never disclose confidential information via direct message; if uncertain, direct users to official support channels.

    • Notification Messages: Automated notifications sent to users, such as confirmations for bookings or newsletter sign-ups, should be clear and respectful.

  9. Platform Policy Compliance

    • Adherence to Platform Policies: Sahel Politica and its representatives must comply with each platform's policies (Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram, etc.), avoiding prohibited behaviors (spam, inappropriate content, etc.).

    • Staying Informed of Policy Changes: Teams are responsible for staying updated on any changes to platform policies to ensure compliance.

  10. Consequences and Disciplinary Action

    Any breach of these guidelines may result in disciplinary action, up to and including suspension or termination, depending on the severity of the infraction.

  11. Code Updates

    This Social Media Code of Conduct may be updated to reflect changes in Sahel Politica policies or social media platform rules. Updates will be communicated to employees and affiliates, and the latest Code will be available on our website.

This Social Media Code of Conduct ensures that Sahel Politica remains a respected professional entity online, while allowing employees to positively contribute to the company’s communications.

Media House Rules for Sahel Politica

Effective Date: October 25, 2024

This document outlines Sahel Politica’s Media House Rules, which apply to all employees, contractors, and representatives when engaging with the media. The purpose is to ensure that Sahel Politica's communications align with its mission, maintain brand consistency, and project a professional image.

  1. Guidelines for Media Engagement

    • Authorization: Only designated spokespersons or approved representatives may speak on behalf of Sahel Politica.

    • Media Requests: All media inquiries, including interview requests, article contributions, and event coverage requests, must be directed to the communications team.

    • Advance Notice: The media team should be given at least 72 hours' notice before any planned media engagement to prepare messaging and gather necessary information.

  2. Interview Protocol

    • Approval Process: Prior to any interview, whether written, audio, or video, employees must obtain approval from the communications department.

    • Content Accuracy: All statements made in interviews should be factually correct, relevant, and reflect the core values of Sahel Politica. Avoid speculative statements or unverified information.

    • Consistent Messaging: Approved key messages and talking points provided by the communications team should be adhered to strictly to ensure message consistency.

  3. Public Statements and Press Releases

    • Press Release Approval: All press releases must be reviewed and approved by the communications team. Unauthorized press releases or public statements are prohibited.

    • Language and Tone: The tone should be professional, factual, and aligned with Sahel Politica’s brand. Avoid exaggerated or ambiguous language.

    • Timing of Release: Release timing should be coordinated with the communications team to maximize engagement and align with strategic goals.

  4. Event Coverage and Media Invitations

    • Event Access: Media must request access to attend Sahel Politica’s events, which will be granted or denied by the communications team.

    • Recording and Photography: Sahel Politica reserves the right to restrict recording and photography during events to protect the privacy of attendees and the confidentiality of discussions.

    • Media Guidelines at Events: Journalists and media representatives must follow Sahel Politica’s guidelines, including respecting the schedule and refraining from disruptive behavior.

  5. Handling Sensitive Topics

    • Prepared Responses: For sensitive topics, the communications team will prepare approved responses. Only designated individuals may address these topics with the media.

    • No Speculation: Avoid speculating or offering personal opinions on issues that could impact Sahel Politica’s reputation or operations.

    • Privacy and Confidentiality: Do not discuss client-specific information, internal policies, or any other confidential matter with the media.

  6. Social Media Guidelines for Media Relations

    • Official Social Media Channels: Media interactions on social media should be managed through Sahel Politica’s official social media channels.

    • Responding to Media on Social Media: If approached by the media on social media, direct them to the communications team for an official response.

    • Respectful Interaction: All interactions on social media with the media should be respectful and in line with Sahel Politica’s Social Media Code of Conduct.

  7. Media Monitoring and Issue Management

    • Monitoring: The communications team will monitor media coverage related to Sahel Politica. Any issues or inaccuracies in media coverage should be reported to the team immediately.

    • Managing Negative Coverage: Negative or incorrect media coverage will be addressed proactively by the communications team. Employees must not respond independently or make public statements.

  8. Visual Assets and Brand Representation

    • Brand Assets: Sahel Politica’s logo, images, and other visual assets may only be provided to media outlets with prior approval from the communications team.

    • Image Usage Restrictions: Unauthorized use of Sahel Politica’s visual assets in media or third-party publications is prohibited.

    • Image Quality and Consistency: Approved images and branding should be used in a way that reflects the quality and professionalism of Sahel Politica.

  9. Crisis Communication

    • Crisis Response Protocol: In the event of a crisis, the crisis response plan will be implemented, and only designated spokespersons will address the media.

    • Internal Notification: Employees must notify the communications team immediately if they are contacted by the media regarding a sensitive or crisis-related issue.

    • Unified Message: Crisis communications will follow a prepared script to maintain message control and prevent misinformation.

  10. Confidentiality and Privacy in Media Relations

    • Client Confidentiality: Sahel Politica prioritizes client privacy. Media communications must not disclose any client information without explicit consent.

    • Employee Privacy: Employee information, aside from designated public profiles, should remain confidential unless an individual has given prior consent.

    • Data Protection Compliance: Sahel Politica complies with all relevant data protection regulations; no personal data should be shared with the media without appropriate consent.

  11. Legal Compliance

    • Intellectual Property: Employees and representatives must ensure that all content shared with the media respects intellectual property rights.

    • No Defamatory Statements: Statements made to the media must not defame or negatively target any individual, competitor, or third-party organization.

    • Compliance with Media Laws: Sahel Politica will comply with local and international media laws when engaging with the press.

  12. Employee Social Media and Media Interaction

    • Role Clarity: Employees who are not official spokespersons should avoid discussing company policies, clients, or projects on social media.

    • No Personal Statements on Company Matters: Employees should avoid making public statements on company matters unless officially authorized.

    • Referring Media to Communications Team: Any media request directed to a non-designated employee should be referred to the communications team.

  13. Review and Updates

    • Policy Review: The Media House Rules will be reviewed regularly by the communications team to ensure relevance and effectiveness.

    • Notification of Changes: Significant changes to this policy will be communicated to all employees and affiliates.

These Media House Rules are intended to provide clear guidelines and expectations for media interactions. By adhering to this code, Sahel Politica’s team ensures that media engagement is handled professionally and strategically, enhancing the company’s reputation and protecting its interests.
